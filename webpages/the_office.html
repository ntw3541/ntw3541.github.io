Network of The Office (US) Characters

[Python Code]

Introduction

Recently, I found The Office (US) transcript dataset from Kaggle, which gave me an opportunity to explore the network relationship among The Office characters through the 9 seasons.

Character Network by Season

The following graphs show character network by season. Each point (node) represents a character. Each line (edge) between two nodes indicates that the two characters have appeared in the same scene. The width of the edge presents the number of times that the two characters shared a scene. The graph applies force-directed graph drawing, which means that characters who have more shared scenes with other characters (greater centrality) are more likely to appear at the center of the graph. Monologue scenes were excluded during the making the network graphs.

Each of the following tabs of the 9 seasons contains two graphs (except for Season 1 and 4). The first graph shows characters that appear in at least 10 non-monologue scenes. For most seasons, including peripheral characters causes the core characters to cram together in the center. Therefore, the second graph only shows characters with at least 40 non-monologue scenes, so that we can get a better sense of the relationship among the core characters. "All Seasons" tab contains 3 graphs to help viewers zoom in from periphery to the central characters.
